# Deep Research Query: Johann Joseph Gassner

## Subject
Johann Joseph Gassner (1727–1779), Catholic priest and healer, known for public exorcism-based healing sessions in 1770s Germany. Considered a direct precursor to Franz Anton Mesmer and the development of hypnosis.

---

## Research Query

Conduct a comprehensive bibliographic investigation of Johann Joseph Gassner, focusing on the following questions:

### 1. Complete Works
- What texts did Gassner himself author or co-author? Provide full bibliographic details for each: title (original language), year of publication, place of publication, publisher, language, and approximate length.
- Were any of his works published in multiple editions? If so, list all known editions with dates and publishers.
- Did he write any letters, pamphlets, or shorter pieces beyond his main published works?
- Are any of his manuscripts known to survive unpublished?

### 2. Contemporary Responses and Polemical Literature
- Which authors wrote in support of Gassner's healing methods? Provide titles, dates, authors, and publishers.
- Which authors wrote against him? Provide the same details.
- What was the official Catholic Church position on his activities, and are there any official documents (episcopal letters, Vatican correspondence) that survive?
- What was the Enlightenment response? Which Aufklärung writers engaged with him specifically?

### 3. The Mesmer Connection
- What is the documented relationship between Gassner and Franz Anton Mesmer?
- Did Mesmer directly observe or engage with Gassner's sessions? When and where?
- How did Mesmer's contemporaries compare the two figures?
- Which specific elements of Gassner's technique appear to have influenced Mesmer's theory of animal magnetism?

### 4. Who Cited Gassner
- Which later writers on mesmerism, magnetism, or hypnosis cited Gassner by name?
- Was Gassner discussed in the major 19th-century histories of hypnosis (e.g., by Braid, Liébeault, Bernheim, Moll, or Janet)?
- Any 20th-century historians of psychology or medicine who wrote specifically about Gassner?

### 5. English Translations
- Have any of Gassner's works been translated into English, either in the 18th–19th century or more recently?
- Are there any partial translations in academic articles or book chapters?

### 6. Key Secondary Sources
- What are the most authoritative scholarly works on Gassner in any language?
- Is there a definitive biography?

### 7. Sourcing
- Which of Gassner's works are available in digitized form (Google Books, Internet Archive, Deutsche Digitale Bibliothek, Bayerische Staatsbibliothek, etc.)?
- Provide direct URLs where possible.

---

## Output Format Requested

Return results as a structured list organized by the seven sections above. For each work identified, use this format:

```
- Author, Title (Original Language Title if different), Year, Publisher, City. Language. [Notes: editions, availability, URL if known]
```

Prioritize completeness over brevity. Include works you are uncertain about, flagged as [unverified].
